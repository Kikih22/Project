Retina-Display

Retina display had its debut when the iPhone 4 came out. Retina display enhanced screen quality and resolution. Retina display added more pixels to the screen allowing more detail in images and a clearer image. Retina display was updated from the iphone 3 to the iphone 4. Retina display also made its debut on the iPad, 3rd generation and iPod,3rd generation. Retina display enhanced the quality of the screens and pixel resolution. Retina display first came out in 2010 and each pixel was split into four pixels. The bigger the screen the more pixels are present. Higher pixel density means higher resolutions and clearer images. Retina display has been expanded to almost all of the Apple products such as iPhones, iPads, Apple watches, and iMacs. There are two softwares connected to retina displays: LCD and OLED. They differ in resolutions, contrast levels, color reproduction, or refresh rates. Retina display varies with the size of the screen. The smaller the device the closer your face is to the screen. When you are looking closely at the screen you see more pixels per inch. This gives a higher resolution. Apple uses different names for different versions of retina. The names are “Retina HD display”, “Retina 4K/5K display", "Retina 4.5K display", "Super Retina HD display", "Super Retina XDR display", and "Liquid Retina display". According to Steve Jobs the number of pixels you need to have retina display is roughly 300 PPI when the device is 10-12 inches from the eye.
